### Long bio — EN

Larissa Sans is a Brazilian visual artist and multidisciplinary designer based in Amsterdam. She holds a degree in Visual Arts from Faculdade Paulista de Artes (FPA, São Paulo) and in Graphic Design from the British School of Creative Arts (EBAC), and has further trained in cinema, classical ballet, and computational graphics.

Her work investigates the relationship of bodies — especially women's — with the environment, with dreams, with memory, and with ancestry. Rooted in São Paulo and shaped by Amsterdam, she moves fluidly between physical media (painting, linoprint) and digital practice (illustration, branding, photography), bringing the same depth of intention to a personal commission and to a brand identity.

She works with individual clients seeking original art and with small-to-medium businesses — particularly in lifestyle, food, hospitality, and music — that want visual identities with character.

## 3. Brand Positioning

### Positioning statement

Larissa Sans is a visual artist and designer who brings a fine arts sensibility to brand-building — creating work that is visually distinct, culturally layered, and rooted in the intimacy of the female experience.

### Target audiences

**1. Art buyers / collectors**
Individuals who want a unique, original piece — a digital illustration, painting, or linoprint — as a commission or as a gift. They value craft, a distinct artistic voice, and thematic depth.

**2. Business clients**
Small and medium-sized brands — particularly in lifestyle, food & drink, hospitality, and music — that want visual identities with personality, not generic templates. They are often founder-led and care about the story behind their brand.

### Differentiators

- Fine arts degree + graphic design degree — rare combination in the freelance market
- Physical and digital media in equal measure (painting and linoprint alongside digital illustration and branding)
- Brazilian cultural perspective living inside a European creative hub
- Thematic depth: work grounded in the body, memory, dreams, and ancestry — not decorative, but meaningful
- Intimate, personal process: questionnaire-led briefing, strategy meeting, collaborative revisions

### Tone of voice

Warm · Poetic · Direct · Curious · Feminine · Culturally aware

### Aesthetic keywords

Organic forms · Earthy and warm palettes · Feminine figures · Dreamlike narratives · Hand-crafted texture · Bold typography · Layered detail · Intimate scale · Brazilian chromatic richness

## 6. Artist Statement

### EN

I make work about bodies — about what it means to inhabit one, to carry one through space and time, through the city and the forest, through memory and dream. My figures are mostly women, mostly in motion, mostly mid-dissolution into something larger than themselves: water, air, root, ancestry.

I grew up in São Paulo and now live in Amsterdam. The distance between those two places lives inside everything I make. The work is an attempt to hold both — the tropical heat and the grey northern light, the past and the present tense, the intimate and the vast.

Whether I am painting, drawing, printing, or designing for a brand, I am always asking the same question: what does this body know that words cannot say?

### PT

Faço trabalhos sobre corpos — sobre o que significa habitar um, carregá-lo pelo espaço e pelo tempo, pela cidade e pela floresta, pela memória e pelo sonho. Minhas figuras são quase sempre mulheres, quase sempre em movimento, quase sempre a meio caminho de se dissolver em algo maior do que elas mesmas: água, ar, raiz, ancestralidade.

Cresci em São Paulo e hoje vivo em Amsterdã. A distância entre esses dois lugares habita tudo que faço. O trabalho é uma tentativa de segurar os dois ao mesmo tempo — o calor tropical e a luz cinza do norte, o passado e o presente, o íntimo e o vasto.

Seja pintando, desenhando, imprimindo ou criando a identidade de uma marca, estou sempre fazendo a mesma pergunta: o que esse corpo sabe que as palavras não conseguem dizer?

## 8. Website Strategy Notes

### Site identity

The website should feel like stepping into a curated artist's studio — not a freelancer marketplace. Art leads; services are a natural extension of the work.

### Pages


| Page      | Purpose                                                                              |
| --------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Home      | Hero with artist statement short-form, featured portfolio pieces, and primary CTA    |
| Portfolio | Full project gallery, filterable by category (Art / Branding / Design / Photography) |
| About     | Long bio, artist statement, education, process philosophy                            |
| Services  | Services catalogue — art commissions, branding packages, content creation            |
| Contact   | Primary enquiry form (links to Google Form or embedded), email, social links         |


### CTAs


| Priority  | CTA (EN)                         | CTA (PT)                          | Destination       |
| --------- | -------------------------------- | --------------------------------- | ----------------- |
| Primary   | "Commission a piece" / "Hire me" | "Encomendar" / "Trabalhar comigo" | Enquiry form      |
| Secondary | "View the work"                  | "Ver o trabalho"                  | Portfolio         |
| Tertiary  | "Follow along"                   | "Acompanhe"                       | Substack / social |


### Tone goal

The copy should sound like Larissa — warm, direct, a little poetic. Not corporate. Not overly casual. The kind of voice that makes someone feel they are working with a person, not a studio.

### Bilingual approach

- Default language: English (primary market is Amsterdam / Europe)
- Portuguese toggle or parallel pages for the Brazilian market
- Artist statement and bio should both appear in full in both languages

### Open items to confirm with Larissa before build

- Hero tagline — 1 sentence she wants above-the-fold on the homepage
- Priority order of services on the Services page (does Branding or Art commissions lead?)
- Whether Substack should be featured as a blog / journal section on the site
- High-resolution portfolio images for each of the 12 projects
- Any projects not yet on Behance that should be included
- Brazil pricing tier (the Brasil Prices PDF was empty — confirm if relevant)
