She grew up in São Paulo. She lives in Amsterdam. In between, she makes work about the body — what it holds, what it forgets, what it carries across borders.

Biography

Larissa Sans is a Brazilian visual artist and multidisciplinary designer based in Amsterdam. She holds a degree in Visual Arts from Faculdade Paulista de Artes (FPA, São Paulo) and in Graphic Design from the British School of Creative Arts (EBAC), and has further trained in cinema, classical ballet, and computational graphics.

Practice

Her work investigates the relationship of bodies — especially women's — with the environment, with dreams, with memory, and with ancestry. Rooted in São Paulo and shaped by Amsterdam, she moves between painting, linoprint, digital illustration, and brand design — bringing the same depth of intention to a personal commission and to a visual identity. She works with individual collectors and with small businesses in lifestyle, food, hospitality, and music.

Art techniques

01

Painting

Working primarily in acrylic and oil, Larissa builds up layers of colour on canvas and board. Her paintings range from small-format intimate studies to larger figurative works. Each begins with loose gestural marks — gradually refined, or deliberately left raw.

Acrylic Oil Mixed Media Canvas

02

Linoprint

Hand-carved from linoleum and printed on paper. The process is irreversible — each cut commits. The resulting works are high-contrast, richly textured, and carry the unmistakable trace of the hand. Available as signed, limited-edition prints.

Hand-carved Linoleum Limited editions

03

Digital Illustration

Working digitally on iPad and desktop, Larissa creates illustrations that retain the warmth and intentionality of hand-drawn work. Used for commissions, brand identities, and editorial projects. Delivered in high resolution — print-ready and screen-ready.
